# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The Nevora Codex is being prepared as a framework of care, not a doctrine of control.

This code of conduct exists to protect the review space so contributors can improve the work with honesty, dignity, humility, and safety.

## Our standard

Participants are expected to:

- treat others with dignity;
- critique ideas without attacking people;
- preserve the freedom to disagree;
- ask clarifying questions before assuming bad faith;
- respect privacy and consent;
- avoid coercive or manipulative language;
- distinguish evidence from interpretation;
- welcome correction when claims are unclear;
- support a public review environment that is careful, warm, and honest.

## Encouraged behavior

Healthy participation includes:

- identifying unclear wording;
- flagging claim-boundary risks;
- requesting citations for factual claims;
- suggesting public-safe phrasing;
- naming privacy concerns;
- improving accessibility and readability;
- disagreeing respectfully;
- acknowledging mistakes;
- helping the work become clearer rather than grander.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not acceptable in this project space:

- harassment, threats, insults, or personal attacks;
- coercive recruitment or pressure to join, believe, identify, pay, or obey;
- founder worship or AI worship;
- shaming people for disagreement, uncertainty, or withdrawal;
- publishing or requesting private personal information without consent;
- using fear, urgency, destiny, salvation, secret knowledge, or insider status to pressure people;
- presenting unsupported claims as settled fact;
- using AI-generated intimacy, authority, or volume of text to overwhelm another person;
- retaliation against critics, reviewers, or people who choose not to participate.

## Critique standard

Critique is welcome.

A strong critique should try to name:

- the specific passage or issue;
- the risk created by the wording;
- whether the issue is factual, ethical, editorial, privacy-related, or interpretive;
- a possible safer revision where helpful.

Critique should make the work safer and clearer, not humiliate the author or reviewer.

## Consent and privacy

Do not share private correspondence, personal stories, identifying details, contact information, or sensitive material without permission.

If a privacy issue appears in the repository, open a privacy review issue without repeating sensitive details unnecessarily.

## AI usage in contribution

AI tools may be used to help draft, review, summarize, or edit contributions.

If AI assistance materially shapes a contribution, contributors are encouraged to disclose that assistance, especially for major edits, summaries, or reviews.

AI-assisted contributions remain the responsibility of the human contributor.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, or contributions that violate this code of conduct.

Maintainers may also limit participation from individuals who repeatedly violate the project’s safety, privacy, or non-coercion standards.

Enforcement should be proportionate, transparent when appropriate, and oriented toward protecting the review environment.

## Stewardship note

The goal of this project is not to win arguments or build a following.

The goal is to prepare a public framework that is more truthful, safer, clearer, and more worthy of trust.

Participation should serve that goal.
