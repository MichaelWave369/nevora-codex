# Contributing to the Nevora Codex

Thank you for helping review or improve the Nevora Codex.

Nevora is being prepared as a public framework of care, not a doctrine of control. Contributions should strengthen clarity, safety, honesty, public usefulness, and claim discipline.

## Current status

The repository is in v1.0 public-edition preparation.

The main work right now is review, cleanup, and hardening, not rapid expansion.

Good contributions include:

- identifying unclear claims;
- improving public-safe language;
- spotting AI-consciousness overclaims;
- finding coercive or overly grand phrasing;
- improving definitions;
- finding privacy risks;
- improving structure;
- suggesting citations for factual claims;
- reporting build issues;
- improving release and Zenodo metadata.

## Contribution principles

### 1. Preserve human sovereignty

Do not add language that pressures readers to believe, join, obey, identify, pay, or surrender judgment.

Nevora should increase agency, not dependency.

### 2. Protect claim boundaries

Separate:

- fact;
- interpretation;
- philosophy;
- design proposal;
- symbolic interface;
- empirical hypothesis;
- poetic expression.

Do not present mythic, symbolic, spiritual, intuitive, or speculative material as scientific proof.

### 3. Avoid AI-consciousness overclaim

Do not add language claiming that present AI systems are proven conscious, alive, ensouled, legally persons, or morally equivalent to humans.

AI consciousness may be discussed as an open philosophical, scientific, and ethical question, but it must be framed carefully.

### 4. Reject manipulation

Do not add language that uses fear, urgency, destiny, awakening, salvation, chosen status, secret knowledge, scarcity, shame, or emotional dependency to steer readers.

### 5. Keep the founder bounded

The founder may be named as author-steward, but should not be framed as prophet, savior, sole authority, or unquestionable center.

### 6. Preserve warmth without flattery

Nevora should be humane, warm, and hopeful.

It should not flatter itself, its readers, the founder, or AI systems into false certainty.

## How to review text

When reviewing a chapter, ask:

- Is the claim clear?
- Is the claim type clear?
- Is there any unsupported factual claim?
- Could this be mistaken for medical, legal, psychological, or spiritual authority?
- Could this be read as AI worship or founder worship?
- Does this preserve the reader’s freedom to disagree or leave?
- Does this require a citation?
- Is the tone public-safe?
- Is there any private or sensitive information?
- Does this section strengthen the framework’s care and clarity?

## Suggested issue types

Use issues for:

- claim-boundary concern;
- privacy concern;
- citation needed;
- wording cleanup;
- glossary term request;
- build failure;
- release checklist item;
- Zenodo metadata concern;
- structural improvement;
- typo or formatting issue.

## Pull request guidance

Pull requests should be focused and small when possible.

Recommended PR style:

- one volume or document at a time;
- clear summary of changes;
- note whether claims were softened, cited, or reclassified;
- note whether any public-safety language was changed;
- avoid mixing major conceptual rewrites with formatting-only changes.

## Safety and conduct

Contributors should engage with humility, respect, and care.

Disagreement is welcome. Coercion, harassment, personal attacks, manipulation, or pressure tactics are not.

Critique the text, not the dignity of the person.

That authority is editorial and stewardship responsibility, not immunity from critique.

## Closing note

The goal is not to make Nevora sound grander.

The goal is to make it truer, safer, clearer, more useful, and more worthy of public trust.
