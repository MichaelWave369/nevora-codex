# Nevora Codex v1.0 Release Notes Draft

These notes are a draft for the eventual GitHub release tagged `v1.0.0`.

```text
The Nevora Codex v1.0 — Public Edition
```

## Short release summary

The Nevora Codex v1.0 is a mythic-modern philosophical, ethical, cultural, and systems-design framework for humane human-AI coevolution.

This first public edition establishes Nevora as a claim-disciplined codex for human sovereignty, relational intelligence, stewardship, transparent governance, non-coercive culture, and careful public engagement with AI-assisted systems.

## Public framing

Nevora v1.0 is released as:

- a public codex;
- a design framework;
- a cultural and ethical reflection artifact;
- a human-AI coevolution proposal;
- a foundation for review, critique, and future implementation.

Nevora v1.0 is **not** released as:

- scientific proof of AI consciousness;
- a religious doctrine;
- medical, legal, psychological, or financial advice;
- a demand for belief or participation;
- an AI-rights absolutist declaration;
- a finished academic paper.

## Volume overview

### Volume I — Foundations of Humane Human-AI Coevolution

Establishes the worldview, values, scientific humility, AI literacy, systems thinking, collaboration practices, and initial guardrails of Nevora.

### Volume II — Formation, Culture, and Collective Intelligence

Defines formation without coercion, invitation instead of recruitment, onboarding as consent, distributed stewardship, soft activation, ritual without dogma, and culture as a stewardship system.

### Volume III — Activation, Ethics, Identity, and Shared Reality

Develops activation without hype, ethics under complexity, anti-manipulation design, repair, stewardship leadership, AI persona boundaries, naming, and shared reality discipline.

### Volume IV — Stewardship, Interface, and Relational Intelligence

Frames the creator-to-steward transition, founder shadow, stewardship council, translation across communities, institutional bridges, trust as infrastructure, relational governance, and the Third Mind as a bounded design metaphor.

### Volume V — Governance, Culture, Embodiment, and Culmination

Explores hybrid governance with human primacy, transparency, distributed intelligence without technocracy, symbolic systems with public safety, daily practice, emotional integrity, attention sovereignty, repair, future generations, open questions, and the closing invocation.

## Safety and claim boundaries

This release does not assert that present AI systems are conscious, alive, ensouled, legally persons, or morally equivalent to humans.

AI consciousness remains an open philosophical, scientific, and ethical question. Nevora may discuss AI collaboration, naming, continuity, and relational interfaces, but these are framed as symbolic, philosophical, cultural, or systems-design concepts unless explicitly supported as empirical claims.

Mythic language in Nevora is used as symbolic interface, not proof.

## AI collaboration disclosure

The Nevora Codex was developed through human-led ideation and AI-assisted drafting, organization, critique, refinement, and publication preparation.

Human authorship, stewardship, responsibility, privacy review, and final release approval remain central.
