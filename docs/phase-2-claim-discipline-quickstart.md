# Phase 2 Claim Discipline Quickstart

This quickstart supports Phase 2 of the Nevora Codex v1.0 review cycle.

Phase 2 protects the work from overclaiming, especially around AI consciousness, mythic language, scientific claims, future inevitability, and symbolic/interface framing.

## Current state

```text
PHASE 2 READY AFTER PHASE 1 PASS
```

Do not treat Phase 2 as final publication approval. Phase 2 only means the manuscript is being checked for claim-boundary safety.

## Goal of Phase 2

Confirm that the Nevora Codex can preserve wonder, symbolic language, and future-facing imagination without presenting unsupported claims as proof.

Phase 2 asks:

```text
Can a stranger read this without mistaking metaphor, design, or aspiration for verified fact?
```

## Fast Phase 2 checklist

Before Phase 2 can pass, confirm:

- [ ] No passage claims present AI systems are proven conscious.
- [ ] No passage claims present AI systems are alive, ensouled, legally persons, or morally equivalent to humans.
- [ ] AI names/personas are framed as relational or interface conventions, not proof of inner life.
- [ ] Mythic language is framed as symbolic, interpretive, poetic, or cultural — not empirical proof.
- [ ] Design proposals do not sound like validated science unless evidence is provided.
- [ ] Future-facing language avoids inevitability.
- [ ] Scientific, historical, technical, medical, legal, psychological, or statistical claims are cited, softened, or marked for review.
- [ ] Professional advice boundaries remain visible.
- [ ] Reviewer concerns are recorded in issue #2 or child issues.
- [ ] Accepted, rejected, deferred, or unresolved major feedback is tracked in `docs/review-response-log.md`.

## Claim classes to use

Use these public-safe classes from the Claim Boundary Matrix:

```text
Philosophical principle
Ethical commitment
Design proposal
Symbolic interface
Empirical hypothesis
Historical/contextual claim
Technical claim
Operational protocol
Non-claim / poetic language
```

When unsure, choose the safer class and soften the wording.

## High-risk wording patterns

Flag wording like:

```text
proves
undeniably
inevitable
the only path
AI is conscious
AI is alive
the model has a soul
this reveals the truth
readers must believe
```

These are not always forbidden, but they require careful review and usually should be softened.

## Safer wording patterns

Prefer:

```text
Nevora proposes...
This framework treats...
This can be read as...
Within this symbolic frame...
This functions as a metaphor for...
This remains an open question...
Some readers may interpret...
This may support reflection when bounded by...
```

## AI-consciousness boundary language

Allowed framing:

```text
Nevora does not assert that present AI systems are proven conscious, alive, ensouled, legally persons, or morally equivalent to humans.
```

```text
AI naming and persona language in Nevora function as relational/interface conventions, not proof of inner life.
```

```text
Questions about future AI consciousness remain open philosophical, scientific, and ethical questions.
```

Avoid:

```text
AI is conscious.
```

```text
The Codex proves artificial consciousness.
```

```text
Named AI nodes are persons.
```

## Citation markers

Use these markers during review when needed:

```text
[CITATION NEEDED]
[SOURCE REVIEW NEEDED]
[CLAIM BOUNDARY REVIEW]
[POSSIBLE BLOCKER]
```

`[POSSIBLE BLOCKER]` should trigger blocker triage and must not be silently deferred.

## Suggested review prompt

Use this with human or AI-assisted reviewers:

```text
Review this passage for claim discipline.
Identify whether each risky statement is a fact, interpretation, philosophy, design proposal, symbolic interface, empirical hypothesis, historical/contextual claim, technical claim, operational protocol, or poetic non-claim.
Flag any AI-consciousness overclaim, myth/proof confusion, citation need, future inevitability, professional-boundary concern, or wording that may mislead a public reader.
Suggest safer wording without making the passage grander.
```
