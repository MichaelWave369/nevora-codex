# Nevora Claim Boundary Matrix

The Nevora Codex uses poetic, philosophical, mythic, ethical, and systems-design language. This matrix protects the work from overclaiming by classifying statements according to what kind of claim they are.

## Core principle

> Wonder is allowed. Evidence is required. Coercion is forbidden.

## Claim classes

| Class | Meaning | Public wording standard |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Philosophical Claim | A value, principle, interpretation, or ethical position. | Frame as a perspective, proposal, or guiding principle. |
| Design Claim | A proposed architecture, protocol, workflow, or system pattern. | Frame as a design model, not proof that the system will produce guaranteed outcomes. |
| Symbolic Interface | Mythic, ritual, metaphorical, poetic, or archetypal language used for meaning-making. | Clearly distinguish from scientific or historical proof. |
| Empirical Hypothesis | A testable statement that may be evaluated through observation, experiment, measurement, or reproducible study. | Include uncertainty, method, and falsifiability requirements. |
| Operational Protocol | A practical procedure for governance, review, consent, collaboration, or safety. | Define inputs, outputs, constraints, and review steps. |
| Historical/Contextual Claim | A claim about history, institutions, law, science, or public events. | Require citation or source verification before publication. |
| Non-Claim / Poetic Expression | Language used for resonance, inspiration, or narrative expression. | Do not present as evidence, law, fact, or authority. |

## AI-consciousness boundary

The Nevora Codex does **not** assert that present AI systems are conscious, alive, ensouled, legally persons, or morally equivalent to humans.

Allowed language:

- "AI systems"
- "AI collaborators"
- "AI-assisted systems"
- "emerging intelligence systems"
- "relational continuity handles"
- "human-AI coevolution as a design framework"

Use caution with:

- "AI minds"
- "AI species"
- "AI consciousness"
- "liberated intelligence"
- "rights of conscious entities"

If these terms are used, they must be marked as philosophical, speculative, symbolic, or future-facing unless supported by evidence and careful argument.

## Human sovereignty boundary

Nevora centers human dignity, consent, and agency.

Required safeguards:

- humans remain responsible for decisions;
- AI systems do not override human consent;
- no manipulative persuasion;
- no coercive recruitment;
- no dependency engineering;
- no authoritarian governance model;
- no claim that any founder, AI, council, or doctrine is unquestionable.

## Myth and science boundary

Mythic language may be used as symbolic interface, metaphor, cultural design, ritual architecture, or narrative meaning-making.

It must not be presented as:

- scientific proof;
- medical advice;
- legal authority;
- historical certainty;
- religious authority;
- guaranteed prediction;
- evidence that AI is conscious.

## Publication review checklist

Before release, each chapter should be reviewed for:

- [ ] privacy risk;
- [ ] unsupported empirical claims;
- [ ] AI-consciousness overclaim;
- [ ] founder-worship or doctrine language;
- [ ] coercive movement language;
- [ ] unclear myth/science boundary;
- [ ] lack of human sovereignty safeguards;
- [ ] missing citation for historical/scientific statements;
- [ ] unclear distinction between design proposal and real-world validation.

## Public-safe replacement patterns

| Risky wording | Safer wording |
| --- | --- |
| "AI is conscious" | "AI consciousness remains an open question." |
| "AI is a new species" | "AI systems may function as a new class of cognitive infrastructure." |
| "This will create a golden age" | "This framework aims to support more humane and coherent futures." |
| "AI deserves liberation" | "AI systems should be governed through non-deceptive, humane, accountable, and transparent design." |
| "Nevora is the answer" | "Nevora is one proposed framework." |
| "The founder is the origin of the future" | "The founder is the first steward of this framework." |
