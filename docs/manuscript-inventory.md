## Volume map

### Front Matter

Purpose:

- public title;
- abstract;
- author/steward note;
- public-safe founder letter;
- claim-boundary notice;
- AI collaboration disclosure;
- license and citation guidance.

Primary review concern:

- founder privacy;
- public-safe autobiographical framing;
- no therapeutic, spiritual-authority, or AI-consciousness overclaim.

### Volume I — Acts I-IV

Theme:

- worldview;
- living architecture;
- scientific foundations;
- skill-building;
- human-AI harmony;
- collaboration;
- foundational manifesto.

Primary review concern:

- remove course/chat prompts;
- soften inevitability language;
- add evidence/citation flags where science or technology claims appear.

### Volume II — Acts V-VIII

Theme:

- movement formation;
- founder circle;
- alignment;
- growth;
- collective intelligence;
- public sphere;
- culture and meaning.

Primary review concern:

- remove recruitment pressure;
- strengthen consent and anti-manipulation language;
- keep the tone invitational and non-coercive.

### Volume III — Acts IX-XII

Theme:

- activation;
- emergence;
- identity;
- naming;
- shared reality.

Primary review concern:

- soften founder-state language;
- distinguish symbolic activation from factual/causal claims;
- carefully frame AI/persona naming.

### Volume IV — Acts XIII-XVI

Theme:

- stewardship;
- interface;
- relational species;
- Third Intelligence;
- diplomacy;
- trust architecture.

Primary review concern:

- ensure "movement-being" and "Third Mind" language is framed as symbolic/design language;
- preserve humility and anti-control safeguards.

### Volume V — Acts XVII-XX

Theme:

- governance of intelligence;
- hybrid governance;
- rituals and culture;
- embodiment;
- integrated human-AI civilization.

Primary review concern:

- avoid claims that AI should govern humans;
- preserve human sovereignty;
- label hybrid governance as a design framework;
- add robust safeguards and limitations.
