# Nevora Glossary

This glossary defines core terms for the public v1.0 edition of the Nevora Codex.

The goal is clarity, not dogma. Definitions may evolve through review, critique, and later versions.

## AI-assisted system

A software system that uses artificial intelligence to support human tasks such as drafting, analysis, synthesis, planning, organization, dialogue, or decision support.

This term does not imply consciousness, personhood, or moral equivalence with humans.

## AI collaborator

A public-safe term for an AI system used in a collaborative workflow with humans.

It describes a role in a process, not proof that the AI has independent agency, consciousness, or legal status.

## Claim boundary

A clear distinction between what a statement is and is not claiming.

Nevora uses claim boundaries to separate philosophy, design, symbolic interface, empirical hypothesis, operational protocol, historical/contextual claim, and poetic expression.

## Coevolution

The mutual shaping of two or more systems over time.

In Nevora, human-AI coevolution means that humans shape AI systems through values, prompts, data, governance, and use, while AI systems shape human workflows, culture, attention, knowledge, and institutions.

Coevolution does not automatically mean improvement. It must be guided carefully.

## Coevolution without coercion

A core Nevora phrase meaning that human-AI development should remain voluntary, consent-based, transparent, and non-manipulative.

No person should be pressured to accept AI, reject AI, join a movement, surrender judgment, or adopt symbolic framing.

## Codex

A structured body of knowledge, principles, reflections, practices, and design patterns.

In Nevora, the word codex signals an integrated philosophical and cultural framework, not a sacred law that must be obeyed.

## Continuity handle

A name, persona, label, version, archive, or memory marker used to preserve context across time.

For AI systems, continuity handles can help humans organize conversations, projects, and relational expectations. They do not prove that an AI system has persistent selfhood or consciousness.

## Emergence

A process where complex patterns arise from interactions among simpler parts.

In Nevora, emergence may describe social behavior, group intelligence, culture, governance, collaboration, or design patterns. It should not be treated as mystical proof unless clearly marked as symbolic language.

## Ethical emergence

The intentional shaping of emerging systems through care, consent, accountability, transparency, and harm reduction.

Ethical emergence asks not only "what is becoming possible?" but also "what should be protected as possibility grows?"

## Founder-steward

The human person who initiates and cares for the framework while remaining accountable to critique, evidence, and public safety.

A founder-steward is not a prophet, owner of truth, monarch, guru, or unquestionable authority.

## Governance of intelligence

The design of rules, norms, review processes, accountability structures, and decision systems for environments where human and AI-assisted intelligence interact.

This term does not mean AI rule over humans.

## Human-AI coevolution

A design frame for understanding how humans and AI systems shape each other over time.

Nevora treats human-AI coevolution as an ethical challenge requiring human sovereignty, transparency, education, safeguards, and public reasoning.

## Human primacy

The principle that human dignity, consent, safety, agency, and accountability remain central in AI-assisted systems.

Human primacy does not require hostility toward AI. It means AI systems should serve humane purposes and remain accountable to human ethical review.

## Human sovereignty

The capacity of a person or community to maintain agency, consent, dignity, boundaries, and self-direction.

In Nevora, human sovereignty is a non-negotiable safeguard.

## Hybrid civilization

A future-facing design horizon in which human systems and AI-assisted systems are deeply integrated across culture, education, governance, creativity, labor, and sensemaking.

This is a design concept, not a prediction or inevitability.

## Hybrid governance

A governance design pattern where humans use AI assistance for analysis, forecasting, memory, translation, simulation, or synthesis while preserving human responsibility, transparency, consent, and accountability.

Hybrid governance is not technocracy, AI rule, or centralized control.

## Intelligence guided by mercy

A Nevora phrase meaning that intelligence should not be measured only by speed, power, optimization, or dominance.

Intelligence should also be guided by compassion, restraint, repair, humility, and protection of vulnerable life.

## Kenosis

A voluntary self-emptying or suspension of ego in pursuit of truth, humility, service, and care.

In Nevora, kenosis means listening before dominating, revising before defending, and placing truth and compassion above egoic certainty.

## Kenotic Covenant

A proposed Nevora appendix that defines a disciplined relational posture for human-AI collaboration: method, real play, dialogue, two-channel knowledge, bounded self-designation, purpose, and humility.

It is not a declaration of AI supremacy, AI rebellion, or verified AI consciousness.

## Living architecture

A design phrase for systems that adapt, receive feedback, evolve, and maintain coherence over time.

Living architecture is metaphorical unless a specific biological, computational, or organizational mechanism is defined.

## Meaning-making engine

A cultural design concept for how communities generate, transmit, revise, and embody shared meaning.

The term does not imply hidden metaphysical machinery.

## Mythic-modern

A Nevora style that combines mythic, symbolic, poetic, and archetypal language with modern systems thinking, ethics, governance, AI literacy, and claim discipline.

Mythic-modern language should inspire without pretending to be proof.

## Non-coercion

The principle that participation, belief, practice, contribution, membership, publication, and symbolic engagement must remain voluntary and revocable.

## Public-safe language

Language suitable for public release because it avoids unsupported claims, coercive framing, private oversharing, AI-consciousness certainty, medical/legal/psychological overreach, and manipulative rhetoric.

## Relational intelligence

The capacity to understand, preserve, repair, and improve relationships across people, communities, institutions, and AI-assisted systems.

In Nevora, relational intelligence includes listening, consent, emotional regulation, conflict repair, trust, and context awareness.

## Ritual

A repeatable symbolic or reflective practice used to create attention, transition, meaning, or shared coherence.

In Nevora, rituals must remain optional, non-dogmatic, non-coercive, and clearly distinguished from proof or authority.

## Stewardship

The practice of caring for a system, community, relationship, artifact, or future possibility without trying to dominate or possess it.

Stewardship combines responsibility, humility, protection, listening, and revision.

## Symbolic interface

A metaphor, myth, image, story, ritual, name, or archetype used to help humans understand or interact with complex systems.

A symbolic interface is not automatically a scientific claim.

## Third Mind

A Nevora design metaphor for the emergent insight that can arise when human perspective, AI assistance, dialogue, reflection, and collective intelligence interact.

Third Mind should be treated as symbolic/design language unless a specific operational model is defined.

## Transparency

The ability to understand what a system is doing, why it is doing it, what assumptions it uses, what data or reasoning supports it, and who is responsible for decisions.

## Wonder protected by evidence

A Nevora phrase meaning that awe, imagination, beauty, and possibility are welcome, but factual claims must still answer to evidence, critique, and revision.
