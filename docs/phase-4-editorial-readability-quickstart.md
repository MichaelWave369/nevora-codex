# Phase 4 Editorial Consistency and Readability Quickstart

This quickstart supports Phase 4 of the Nevora Codex v1.0 review cycle.

Phase 4 makes the manuscript readable, consistent, navigable, and durable for public readers who do not know the private origin story behind the work.

## Current state

```text
PHASE 4 READY AFTER PHASES 1-3 PASS OR ACTIVE CLEARANCE
```

Phase 4 is not a claim-boundary replacement. Claim and safety concerns found during editorial review should still route back to Phase 2 or Phase 3.

## Goal of Phase 4

Confirm that the Nevora Codex is clear enough for a new reader to understand without private context, hidden explanation, or author-steward commentary.

Phase 4 asks:

```text
Can the manuscript carry its meaning clearly when the author is not in the room?
```

## Fast Phase 4 checklist

Before Phase 4 can pass, confirm:

- [ ] Volume titles are consistent across README, table of contents, source files, and site text.
- [ ] Chapter and act numbering are consistent.
- [ ] Headings use a predictable hierarchy.
- [ ] The front matter explains what Nevora is and what it is not.
- [ ] Repeated phrases are intentional, not accidental clutter.
- [ ] Glossary terms match manuscript usage.
- [ ] New readers can understand the project without private chat context.
- [ ] Dense sections are broken into readable paragraphs.
- [ ] Overly grand language is simplified where clarity improves.
- [ ] Editorial revisions do not weaken claim boundaries or safety boundaries.
- [ ] Links and file paths are correct.
- [ ] Typos, punctuation issues, and formatting inconsistencies are resolved or logged.

## Editorial review questions

Ask:

- Is this sentence clear on first read?
- Does this paragraph have one main idea?
- Does this section repeat something already said nearby?
- Does this term appear before it is defined?
- Does this heading accurately describe the section?
- Does this passage sound more grand than useful?
- Would a skeptical reader understand the intended boundary?
- Would a reviewer know where to report a concern?

## Common editorial issues

Flag:

```text
unclear referents
long sentence chains
undefined internal terms
inconsistent capitalization
inconsistent volume names
mismatched table of contents entries
repeated motto lines without purpose
private-context assumptions
too much grandeur before explanation
poetic wording where boundary wording is needed
```

## Safe editorial principles

When revising, preserve:

- author-steward voice;
- warmth;
- public-safe boundaries;
- claim discipline;
- non-coercion;
- human sovereignty;
- mythic language where clearly framed;
- clarity over spectacle.

Do not revise the text to sound more authoritative than it is.

## Suggested edit pattern

Use this pattern for suggested revisions:

```text
Original:

Concern:

Suggested revision:

Reason:

Release impact: minor / major / blocker
```

## Suggested review prompt

Use this with human or AI-assisted reviewers:

```text
Review this passage for editorial clarity and public readability.
Look for unclear sentences, repetition, inconsistent terminology, missing definitions, heading problems, private-context assumptions, and overly grand wording.
Suggest concise revisions that preserve the author-steward voice, warmth, claim boundaries, safety boundaries, and human sovereignty.
Do not make the passage grander or more certain.
```

## Phase 4 decision options

Use one:

```text

```

```text

```

```text
PHASE 4 NEEDS REVISION — Readability or consistency issues require revision before release candidate work.
```

```text
PHASE 4 BLOCKED — A major editorial/structural issue prevents release candidate work.
```
