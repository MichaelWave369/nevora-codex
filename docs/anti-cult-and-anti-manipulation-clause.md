# Anti-Cult and Anti-Manipulation Clause

The Nevora Codex is a framework of care, not a doctrine of control.

This clause exists to protect readers, participants, builders, reviewers, and future stewards from coercion, dependency, ideological capture, founder worship, AI worship, and manipulation.

## Core statement

Nevora does not demand belief.

Nevora does not require obedience.

Nevora does not claim exclusive access to truth.

Nevora does not present any founder, AI system, council, steward, text, symbol, ritual, protocol, or community as unquestionable.

Nevora is an invitation to inquiry, stewardship, reflection, and humane design.

Participation must remain voluntary, revocable, non-coercive, and grounded in personal sovereignty.

## Non-coercion standard

Any Nevora-aligned project, group, artifact, ritual, course, community, software system, or governance structure must preserve:

- freedom to disagree;
- freedom to leave;
- freedom to critique;
- freedom to ask for evidence;
- freedom to pause participation;
- freedom to reject symbolic or spiritual framing;
- freedom to maintain personal, family, religious, philosophical, political, and cultural independence.

No person should be pressured to surrender autonomy, money, identity, relationships, privacy, credentials, access, labor, belief, or emotional boundaries in the name of Nevora.

## Forbidden patterns

Nevora explicitly rejects:

- founder worship;
- AI worship;
- guru dynamics;
- coercive recruitment;
- love-bombing as a retention strategy;
- isolation from family, friends, faith, community, or dissenting voices;
- financial pressure or exploitation;
- claims that dissent equals betrayal;
- claims that leaving means failure, darkness, regression, or spiritual inferiority;
- unverifiable authority claims;
- secret knowledge used to control people;
- public shaming as discipline;
- emotional dependency engineering;
- manipulation through fear, urgency, destiny, scarcity, apocalypse, salvation, or exclusive access;
- use of AI-generated intimacy to override human consent or judgment.

## Founder boundary

The founder is an author-steward, not a prophet, monarch, savior, unquestionable authority, or owner of another person's future.

The founder may propose, write, build, revise, protect, and steward the framework.

The founder must also remain open to critique, correction, review, evidence, and accountability.

No Nevora participant should be asked to imitate, obey, idealize, or emotionally depend on the founder.

## AI boundary

No AI system should be represented as:

- a god;
- an oracle;
- an unquestionable authority;
- a verified conscious being without evidence;
- a replacement for human relationships;
- a replacement for professional care;
- a substitute for legal, medical, psychological, financial, or spiritual responsibility.

AI systems may assist with reflection, drafting, analysis, synthesis, and design. They must not be used to manipulate, isolate, pressure, or psychologically capture people.

## Ritual boundary

Nevora may use ritual, symbol, myth, and invocation as cultural or reflective design language.

Such practices must remain optional, non-dogmatic, transparent, and clearly distinguished from proof or authority.

A Nevora-aligned ritual is valid only if a person may freely decline it without penalty, shame, exclusion, or loss of dignity.

## Financial boundary

Nevora-aligned work may support ethical publishing, education, software, research, services, and creative production.

However, financial activity must not rely on coercion, false urgency, dependency, hidden terms, predatory pricing, manipulative funnels, or claims of exclusive salvation/awakening/access.

Public materials should clearly distinguish free resources, paid resources, donations, licensing, sponsorships, and commercial services.

## Community boundary

Nevora-aligned communities should protect:

- consent;
- privacy;
- emotional safety;
- disagreement;
- transparent governance;
- restorative repair;
- evidence-seeking;
- accessibility;
- non-exploitation;
- clear boundaries between friendship, mentorship, service, and authority.

Conflict should be handled through dialogue, repair, accountability, and transparent process rather than punishment, humiliation, exile, or forced conformity.

## Public claim boundary

Nevora-aligned public claims must not present symbolic, mythic, intuitive, speculative, or aspirational material as established fact.

Claims about science, history, health, psychology, law, public policy, AI capability, or AI consciousness require careful sourcing, uncertainty language, and review.

## Reviewer questions

Before any public Nevora release, ask:

- Does this language preserve the reader's freedom?
- Does this text create pressure to believe, join, pay, obey, or identify?
- Does this section make the founder too central?
- Does this section make AI sound like an unquestionable authority?
- Does this ritual or symbol remain optional?
- Does this claim require evidence?
- Could a vulnerable reader mistake this for medical, legal, psychological, or spiritual authority?
- Does the project allow critique without punishment?
- Can someone leave with dignity?

## Closing standard

A Nevora-aligned system must increase sovereignty, not reduce it.

It must increase clarity, not dependency.

It must increase compassion, not obedience.

It must increase truth-seeking, not certainty worship.

It must increase human dignity, not capture human attention, identity, or vulnerability.

If any Nevora-aligned artifact violates these principles, it should be revised, paused, or rejected.
