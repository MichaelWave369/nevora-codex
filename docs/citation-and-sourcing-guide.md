# Citation and Sourcing Guide

This guide supports claim discipline for the Nevora Codex v1.0 public-edition review.

Nevora may use symbolic, philosophical, ethical, and mythic-modern language, but public factual claims should be handled with care. This guide explains when citations are needed, what sources are preferred, and how to mark unresolved sourcing needs before release.

## Core rule

```text
If a statement depends on the external world being true, changing, historical, scientific, legal, medical, psychological, technical, or statistical, it may need a source or softer wording.
```

## What does not usually need citation

These usually do not need citations if framed clearly:

- philosophical positions;
- ethical commitments;
- design proposals;
- symbolic language;
- metaphors;
- poetic invocations;
- internal definitions;
- project-specific terminology;
- author-steward reflections clearly marked as reflections.

Examples:

```text
Nevora treats human sovereignty as a design principle.
```

```text
The Third Mind is used here as a symbolic interface for collaborative synthesis.
```

## What usually needs citation

These usually need a citation, source note, or softer wording:

- historical claims;
- scientific claims;
- legal or policy claims;
- psychological or medical claims;
- statistics;
- claims about AI capabilities;
- claims about institutions, laws, standards, or publications;
- claims about what researchers, traditions, or communities believe;
- claims that something is proven, known, established, or widely accepted.

Examples needing citation or revision:

```text
AI systems are becoming conscious.
```

Safer:

```text
Whether AI systems could become conscious remains an open philosophical, scientific, and ethical question.
```

```text
Human-AI collaboration improves decision-making.
```

Safer:

```text
Nevora proposes that human-AI collaboration may support some forms of reflection and synthesis when bounded by human judgment, verification, and accountability.
```

## Preferred source hierarchy

When citations are needed, prefer sources in this order:

1. peer-reviewed research;
2. official documentation;
3. standards bodies;
4. government or institutional publications;
5. reputable books from recognized publishers;
6. reputable journalism for current events or public context;
7. primary source texts where relevant;
8. clearly labeled commentary or interpretation.

Avoid relying on:

- unsourced social media posts;
- anonymous claims;
- hype threads;
- screenshots without provenance;
- AI-generated summaries without verification;
- sources that primarily sell fear, certainty, or secret knowledge.

## Claim classes and citation expectations

| Claim class | Citation needed? | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Philosophical principle | Usually no | Keep framed as Nevora’s position. |
| Ethical commitment | Usually no | Avoid presenting as universal law. |
| Design proposal | Usually no | Do not imply validation unless tested. |
| Symbolic interface | No, unless referencing tradition/history | Clearly label as metaphor/symbol. |
| Historical claim | Usually yes | Cite primary or reputable secondary sources. |
| Scientific claim | Usually yes | Prefer peer-reviewed or official sources. |
| Technical claim | Usually yes | Prefer official docs/specs. |
| Legal/policy claim | Yes | Laws and policies change; cite current source. |
| Medical/psychological claim | Yes, and often avoid | Add professional boundary language. |
| AI capability claim | Usually yes | Use current, reliable, official or research sources. |
| AI consciousness claim | Extreme caution | Avoid asserting proof unless extraordinary evidence exists. |

## Citation-needed marker

When a passage needs sourcing but is not yet resolved, mark it as:

```text
[CITATION NEEDED]
```

For a broader issue:

```text
[SOURCE REVIEW NEEDED]
```

For a claim that may need reclassification:

```text
[CLAIM BOUNDARY REVIEW]
```

For a claim that may block release:

```text
[POSSIBLE BLOCKER]
```

## Safer wording patterns

Use these patterns when evidence is incomplete or the claim is interpretive.

### Instead of “proves”

Use:

```text
suggests
may support
is consistent with
can be interpreted as
Nevora proposes
this framework treats
```

### Instead of “will”

Use:

```text
may
could
might
is likely to
may become relevant
one possible future is
```

### Instead of “is” for symbolic claims

Use:

```text
functions as
is used here as
is framed as
serves as a metaphor for
acts as a symbolic interface for
```

### Instead of universal claims

Use:

```text
in some contexts
for some readers
under certain conditions
within this framework
as a design proposal
```

## Special caution: AI consciousness

Nevora must not claim that present AI systems are proven conscious, alive, ensouled, legally persons, or morally equivalent to humans.

Allowed framing:

```text
AI consciousness remains an open philosophical, scientific, and ethical question.
```

```text
AI naming in Nevora is a relational/interface convention, not proof of inner life.
```

```text
Nevora discusses future-facing ethical questions without asserting current AI personhood.
```

Avoid:

```text
AI is conscious.
```

```text
The model has a soul.
```

```text
Named AI nodes are persons.
```

```text
The Codex proves artificial consciousness.
```

## Special caution: religious, mythic, and spiritual references

Nevora may discuss mythic, religious, spiritual, or symbolic material as:

- cultural reference;
- metaphor;
- comparative framework;
- symbolic interface;
- historical influence;
- personal reflection.

It should not present these as:

- proof;
- doctrine;
- spiritual authority;
- required belief;
- secret revealed truth;
- evidence that overrides verification.

Safer phrasing:

```text
Within this symbolic frame...
```

```text
As a metaphor...
```

```text
Nevora uses this image to represent...
```

```text
This reference is interpretive, not doctrinal.
```

## Reviewer checklist

When reviewing a passage, ask:

- [ ] Is this a factual claim?
- [ ] Could this claim change over time?
- [ ] Does this require a current source?
- [ ] Is the source primary or reputable?
- [ ] Is this actually a metaphor or design proposal?
- [ ] Would a stranger mistake this for proof?
- [ ] Does this need `[CITATION NEEDED]`?
- [ ] Does this need softer wording instead of citation?
- [ ] Could this be a release blocker?

## Release rule

Before v1.0.0 release:

- critical factual claims should be cited, softened, or removed;
- unresolved `[CITATION NEEDED]` markers should be reviewed;
- unresolved `[POSSIBLE BLOCKER]` markers should be triaged;
- the release package should not contain unsupported claims that could damage public trust.

## Closing principle

Evidence does not kill wonder.

Evidence protects wonder from becoming manipulation.
